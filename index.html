For intimate groups of 4–14 guests. Quiet luxury, generous flavours and the feeling of being welcomed into an Indonesian home.

Yuli started cooking at home and something changed. Packed with flavour, nothing aggressive, sambal always on the side. She took Nic to her favourite warungs. When he tasted something new, he asked her to cook her version, or he asked his kitchen team. He still does.

What came out of that, years of tasting, asking, going back for more, wasn't fusion. It was two kitchens held in the same hands. A translation of European style with Indonesian soul.

Yuli grew up making pindang the way her family always made it, with tomat ranti, a small sour tomato her mother added to the broth that you won't find in any recipe book. She still adds it. It's the difference between pindang and her pindang.

Our Story

Yuli carries the memory. Nic carries the craft.

More than a decade ago, Yuli began introducing Nic to Indonesia’s rich culinary heritage — sharing the family recipes, regional traditions, local ingredients, and stories she grew up with.

While Nic brings over 30 years of international culinary experience and refined fine-dining craftsmanship, Yuli remains the heart of the flavours and the cultural soul behind every dish. Together, they invite guests into an authentic Indonesian dining experience that blends tradition, hospitality, and thoughtful refinement.
